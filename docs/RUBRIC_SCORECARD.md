# Rubric Scorecard Mapping

Use this as your internal speaking checklist during final prep.

## 1) Business Understanding and Problem Clarity (15)
What to say:
- Farmer losses come from delayed action and disconnected decisions.
- Disease, storage, and market are one continuous workflow.
Evidence to show:
- Problem statement slide
- Unified dashboard flow

## 2) Architecture and Technical Design (15)
What to say:
- Modular architecture: ingestion, inference, decision engine, presentation.
- Reliability endpoints and clear data flow.
Evidence to show:
- Architecture diagram
- /health and /presentation-status outputs

## 3) Domain Relevance (10)
What to say:
- Commodity-aware market logic and storage suitability
- Local-language recommendation support
Evidence to show:
- Commodity selector
- Multilingual advisory switch

## 4) Scalability (10)
What to say:
- API-first design supports more devices and districts.
- Planned MQTT and cloud analytics layer.
Evidence to show:
- Endpoint design and modular service files

## 5) Security and Compliance Awareness (10)
What to say:
- Secret management via env, no key in UI
- Planned access controls and auditability
Evidence to show:
- backend/.env.example
- Security roadmap slide

## 6) Innovation (20)
What to say:
- End-to-end farm intelligence in one platform
- Spoilage-aware market decision logic
- Offline fallback for real-world reliability
Evidence to show:
- Market decision card and reason
- Fallback source transparency (live/demo)

## 7) Implementation Feasibility (10)
What to say:
- Working prototype with device firmware and backend scripts
- Simple deployment with one command
Evidence to show:
- scripts/start_backend.ps1
- esp32/field_data_node and esp32/storage_data_node

## 8) Presentation and Documentation (10)
What to say:
- Project is documented for setup, run, architecture, and demo flow
Evidence to show:
- docs/PROJECT_DOCUMENTATION.md
- docs/PRESENTATION_GUIDE.md

## Final Tip
Open this order in your presenter notes and keep transitions explicit:
"Now moving to Scalability...", "Now addressing Security...".
That helps judges map your speech directly to scoring sections.
